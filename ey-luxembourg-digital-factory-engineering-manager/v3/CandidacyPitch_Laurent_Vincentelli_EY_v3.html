Technical Stewardship

I will operationalize and safeguard the engineering standards of the EY Luxembourg Digital Factory through two disciplines: a modern SDLC anchored in DevOps, and architectural stewardship anchored in enterprise reference architectures, reuse, and tech-debt control.

  • SDLC axis (DevOps): oversee the consistent adoption of a high-velocity SDLC: trunk-based development, code reviews, CI/CD, automated testing, Infrastructure as Code as the default pipeline pattern.
  • SDLC axis (DevSecOps + FinOps): enforce systemic integration of shift-left security (SAST/DAST, dependency and secret scanning) and cost-aware build practices (FinOps tagging discipline, budget gates) baked into every pipeline.
  • Architecture axis (framework): steward the Digital Factory's adherence to reference architectures (cloud, microservices) by institutionalizing the systematic use of Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) to ensure traceability and long-term architectural integrity.
  • Architecture axis (reuse + debt): champion reuse across the architectural runway through shared libraries, components, and templates; systematically remediate defects and technical debt to keep solutions maintainable and cost-efficient.

  • reuse rate on new builds lifted from <30% to 70%

Quality, Reliability & Operability

I will drive operational excellence across the EY Luxembourg Digital Factory, keeping it FSI compliant and audit-ready at all times through two disciplines: SRE for quality and reliability, and Platform Engineering for production readiness and self-service operability.

  • SRE axis (observability): champion observability practices (logs, metrics, traces) and codify quality into SLOs, SLIs, and error budgets that drive engineering decisions.
  • SRE axis (incident discipline): lead a blameless incident culture with runbooks and structured post-incident reviews that translate every outage into durable systemic improvements and toil reduction.
  • Platform axis (self-service): champion an internal developer platform with golden paths, self-service infrastructure, and run-time FinOps controls that lift the whole engineering organisation.
  • Platform axis (readiness): orchestrate production readiness, launch reviews, and hypercare in partnership with SMEs, product and support functions to ensure stable operations.
